Customs & Symbols: How the USA Celebrates Halloween

Let’s uncover the traditions that make Halloween a standout celebration in the United States.

Pumpkins Everywhere

From decorations to desserts, pumpkins dominate the Halloween season. This tradition stems from the Irish legend of Jack O'Lantern, the crafty blacksmith who tricked the devil but was barred from both heaven and hell. Left to wander the earth with his lantern, Jack’s tale inspired the practice of carving lanterns.

Initially, turnips were used in Ireland, but in America, pumpkins—larger and easier to carve—became the new standard. Their bright orange color and abundance during fall sealed their place as a Halloween icon.

Pumpkin Carving

Carving pumpkins into Jack O'Lanterns is a beloved tradition, symbolizing the spirit of Halloween.

Pumpkin Spice

Pumpkin spice flavors dominate seasonal treats, from lattes to pies, celebrating the harvest.

Over-the-Top Decorations

Americans don’t just carve pumpkins—they transform their homes into haunted masterpieces. From spiderwebs and skeletons to makeshift graveyards, the Halloween decorating craze covers windows, fences, trees, and lawns. The classic colors orange and black represent the autumn harvest and death, respectively, amplifying the spooky atmosphere.

Haunted Houses

Many neighborhoods create elaborate haunted houses, attracting thrill-seekers and families alike.

Spooky Lights

Decorative lights in orange and purple create a festive and eerie ambiance during Halloween.

Costumes Galore

Halloween costumes are a beloved tradition that echoes the Samhain custom of disguising oneself to evade spirits. Early 20th-century pranksters also donned masks to conceal their identities.

Today, costumes have evolved into a creative and attention-grabbing art form. The scarier or more extravagant the outfit, the better! Many costumes even blend horror with playful sex appeal, showcasing an undeniably American twist.

Costume Contests

Many communities host costume contests, rewarding the most creative and spooky outfits.

DIY Costumes

Crafting homemade costumes is a popular activity, allowing for creativity and personalization.

Trick-or-Treating

The phrase "trick or treat" captures the spirit of American Halloween. Children, dressed in costume, visit house after house demanding candy. This practice likely derives from the Celtic belief that leaving food outside appeased wandering spirits.

By the 1950s, the candy industry capitalized on the holiday, creating specialized sweets for Halloween. Today, sugary staples like candy corn and candy apples ensure that costumed visitors are rewarded for their efforts.

Candy Corn

This iconic Halloween candy is a staple in many households during the season.

Trick-or-Treat Bags

Children often carry themed bags to collect their candy, adding to the festive spirit.

Halloween Parties

Halloween parties have become a popular way for adults and children alike to celebrate the holiday. These gatherings often feature themed decorations, spooky snacks, and costume contests. From haunted house parties to pumpkin carving contests, the festive spirit is alive and well in homes across the country.

Spooky Snacks

Creative treats like "witches' fingers" and "mummy dogs" are popular at Halloween parties.

Halloween Games

Games like bobbing for apples and Halloween trivia add fun to the celebrations.

Brazilian Halloween Traditions

BRAZIL

  1. Saci Pererê Day
    • Folklore Celebration: A very Brazilian twist on Halloween is the celebration of Saci Pererê, a beloved character from Brazilian folklore. Saci is a mischievous, one-legged creature known for playing pranks, wearing a red cap, and causing harmless trouble. On October 31st, Saci Pererê Day is celebrated alongside Halloween, especially in regions that embrace Brazilian traditions.
    • Activities: Children might dress up as Saci or other folklore characters like Cuca (a witch), Curupira (a forest guardian), or Boitatá (a fire serpent). There are storytelling sessions where families gather to share Saci’s tales and other folklore legends, keeping the Brazilian mythical world alive during Halloween.
  2. Haunted Houses and Horror Experiences
    • Brazilian Horror Shows: While theme parks like Hopi Hari and Beto Carrero World feature Halloween-themed events, there are also pop-up haunted houses that take a Brazilian horror approach. These might feature not just international ghosts, but folklore monsters like Zé do Caixão, a character from Brazilian horror films.
    • Immersive Experiences: Some cities, like São Paulo, organize immersive horror experiences where participants are fully engaged in spooky scenarios inspired by Brazilian myths and horror films. This could include a haunted forest experience based on A Ilha do Medo, a Brazilian horror story, or visits to abandoned colonial houses that are said to be haunted by ghosts from Brazil’s history.

Saci Pererê Day

A celebration of the mischievous one-legged character from Brazilian folklore, Saci Pererê, on October 31st.
